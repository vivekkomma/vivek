Obesity - Based on Body Mass Index (BMI)

Obesity is a medical condition characterized by excessive body fat that can have a negative impact on health. It is commonly assessed using the Body Mass Index (BMI), a simple measurement that uses a person's weight and height to estimate if they are within a healthy weight range.

  • Obesity and its Health Risks

    Obesity is associated with a number of serious health risks, including:

    • Heart Disease: Increased risk of coronary artery disease, heart attacks, and heart failure.
    • Type 2 Diabetes: Obesity is a leading risk factor for insulin resistance and diabetes.
    • Hypertension: High blood pressure is often found in obese individuals.
    • Sleep Apnea: Obesity increases the risk of sleep apnea and other breathing issues during sleep.
    • Joint Problems: Excess weight places stress on joints, leading to conditions like osteoarthritis.
    • Cancer: Obesity is linked to higher risks for various types of cancer, including breast, colon, and endometrial cancer.
    • Fatty Liver Disease: Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease is more common in people with obesity.

FAQs

It is advisable to consult a doctor if your BMI falls in the overweight or obese categories (BMI 25), especially if you have other health concerns like high blood pressure, diabetes, or joint pain. Your doctor can help you develop a personalized plan to manage weight and reduce health risks.
